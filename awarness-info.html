Cybersecurity Awareness Guide

🔐 Password Security

Strong passwords are the first line of defense. Always use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password across multiple accounts.

🎣 Phishing Awareness

Phishing attacks trick users into revealing personal information. Always verify links and avoid clicking unknown emails or messages.

🔒 Privacy Protection

Protect your personal information by limiting what you share online. Always keep your social media accounts private.

🌐 Safe Internet Use

Avoid using public WiFi for sensitive tasks like banking. Always check website authenticity before entering credentials.

📱 Device Safety

Regular updates and trusted applications help protect your device from malware and cyber attacks.

Detailed Awareness Based on Quiz Questions

1. Using a password, PIN, or biometric lock protects your device from unauthorized access. Without it, anyone can misuse your personal data.

2. Using the same password for multiple accounts increases risk. If one account is hacked, all accounts become vulnerable.

3. Password managers securely store and generate strong passwords, making it easier to manage multiple accounts safely.

4. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security, making it harder for hackers to access your account.

5. Knowing how to respond to hacking incidents (changing passwords, enabling security settings) can help you recover your account quickly.

6. Regular updates fix security vulnerabilities and protect your device from new cyber threats.

7. Checking app permissions ensures that apps do not misuse your personal data unnecessarily.

8. Giving unnecessary permissions can expose sensitive information like contacts, photos, and microphone access.

9. Reading terms and conditions helps you understand how your data will be used and protects your privacy.

10. Allowing constant location access can compromise your privacy and security.

11. Clicking suspicious links can lead to phishing attacks and malware infections.

12. Using untrusted apps or websites may expose your data to hackers.

13. Keeping social media accounts private helps control who can see your information.

14. Sharing personal information with strangers can lead to identity theft and fraud.

15. Reporting cybercrime helps authorities take action and prevent further incidents.

Did You Know?

🔹 Over 80% of cyber attacks happen due to weak passwords.

🔹 Phishing attacks are one of the most common cyber threats worldwide.

🔹 Public WiFi networks are highly insecure for sensitive transactions.

Best Practices

  • Use strong and unique passwords
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
  • Do not click suspicious links
  • Keep software updated
  • Protect personal information
